# Jay's Takeaway

I used to work in an awesome DevOps environment with amazing SWEs at my last job, so I never needed to worry about iterative product changes on the development side. But in my new job, I have an expectation to deliver fast and no longer have SWEs to rely on so I have to figure out how to push updates faster and ship products on my own. 

Going forward I'll use GitHub to maintain different versions of my analytics products and just have a more efficient way of making updates, tracking changes, and recovering things when they break. I also think this helps me collaborate with stakeholders in the "build the plane while flying" work environment I'm currently situated in.
